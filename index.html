About Me.


I'm a curious and driven student working at the intersection of computational science and scientific research to share my ideas with the world. A recent high school graduate from Nusrat Senior Secondary School with BIG tech wins. Right now, I’m a Data Science Intern at the Medical Research Council Unit The Gambia, part of the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine. Before this, I served as the Lead AI and Machine Learning Intern at Obentas Global Technology Company.
My love for tech started early. I found myself asking existential questions about how computational methods can solve everyday problems. This passion led me to start learning about web development and machine learning, and I soon realized that I could use my skills to create meaningful projects that would make a difference.
During high school, I started tech and research based initiatives. One of my proudest moments was publishing a research paper titled Lowel AI: Using Machine Learning Algorithms and BLA Sensors to Detect Harmful Bacteria in Water, an AI device that aims to detect harmful bacteria present in water. I'm also working on launching StemBia, an initiative focused on using computational science including AI techniques and engineering to solve real challenges in The Gambia. At Nusrat Senior Secondary, I led the ICT Club as both President and lead instructor during the 2023/2024 academic year.
Outside of school and work, I run a YouTube channel called CBJtech, where I share insights on programming, AI, and everything tech.
When not coding, working on a research or working on tech related, I enjoy playing sport, spending time with family and eating too much chocolate. I currently reside in The Gambia.
